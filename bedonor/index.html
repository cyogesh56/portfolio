BeDonor© UX R&D

An Android app UX and Research Review to solve the problem of finding blood, donors and receivers around the world.

Illustrator

Photoshop

Premiere Pro

Problem Statement

Healthcare sector around the world is very scattered when it comes to data and user experience. One of such fields is Blood Donation. Currently, there are millions of non-profit organizations around the world which provide services around blood donation, but when it comes to finding donors and receivers, all of the services fall short whenever an emergency happens. This is because there is one cohesive experience for users where they can find any type of blood donors and receivers ranging from most common to rarest of the rare. Also, users do not use these online services most often when they need certain type of blood or want to donate blood. This is partly because of non-user-friendly apps and websites and not following proper design techniques, leading to loss of users.

Overview

BeDonor is a conceptual application that I first designed in 2014 following Google’s Material Design. The idea of this concept was to democratize the process of not just donating blood but also empower both the receiver and blood banks to receive and manage blood and inventory respectively. This project was extended with updated design and improved interface in 2015. This is the third version with complete overhaul of design using tried and tested design methods based on research review of Non-profit websites and apps and iOS Human Machine Interaction Guidelines.

Iterations

Bedonor (1.0)

2014

The initial concept is based on the idea that there are people who are willing to donate blood via Blood Donation Drives happening in their cities and there are blood banks which have their own records and are not centralized. Hence there is a need to create a centralized platform for everyone in the loop to easily, donate, receive and manage blood and related resources.

BeDonor Extended (2.0)

2015

As I completed this idea, I found out that the Blood Donation Drive model is not sustainable enough because it requires a lot of investment of time and money from Hospitals and Blood Banks, and is a hassle for donors. So, this idea need a refinement. Then I looked into the social aspect of giving and taking blood. I found out that the journey of blood from donor’s body to receiver’s body is very lengthy, time-consuming and requires a lot of steps from various ends for the person in need to get desired group of blood.

Features of v2.0

  • Ability for the user to find, contact and navigate to Donors, Blood Banks, Clinics, Hospitals.

  • Ability for the user to search a blood type in a blood bank’s inventory and book a pick-up.

  • Detailed information about a blood bank’s facilities, amenities, ratings, reviews and contact information.

BeDonor Reimagined (3.0)

2018

I revisited this concept in 2018 through a Research Review on the topic of “Aesthetics and Social factors of Non-Profit Websites and Apps”. Under the research review I reviewed peer-reviewed scholarly journals which tested various aesthetics aspects of website and apps such as colors, layout, fonts, images and shapes, and social aspects such as satisfaction, gratification, credibility, etc. made by non-profit organizations.

Aesthetical Factors

  • Color

  • Layout

  • Imagery

  • Fonts

  • Shapes

Social Factors

  • Satisfaction

  • Gratification

  • Motivation

  • Credibility

  • Gamification

  • Feedback

Conclusion of Research Findings

  • Bright-warm colors and circular elements can create a visual harmony if used properly.

  • Images of actual scenarios look attractive and influential.

  • Users need brief and most relevant information, in combination with visual cues such as icons and images.

  • Emojis can be used to give feedback to users on their progress and can be a crucial part of the gamification process.

  • Awarding users with some achievements gives them satisfaction of achieving goals and keep them motivated.

Visual Design for BeDonor Reimagined

Features of v3.0

  • Clean, accessibility-friendly design that is based on concrete research and tests appealing to people interested in donating to non-profit organizations.

  • Gamified interactions that awards users on completing achievements and proven to increase the app retention rate.

  • Informative interface that keeps users informed of their last donation, upcoming donation and remaining days to next donation.

  • Easy and clutter-free navigation system which allows the user to get what they need in fewer steps.

Scope of future work

  • Contextual Inquiry - Conducting Contextual Inquiry with people who have already donated blood about their past experiences and people who have never donated blood about their reasons of not being a blood donor.

  • Journey Mapping - Identify the journey of the user from willing to donate blood to completing his first achievement of donating and the journey of a person searaching blood to getting transfused and completing the achievement.

  • Task Flows - Creating task flows for Blood Donor and Blood Receiver. Then designing a prototype for usability testing.

  • Usability studies - Conduct formative usability study to validate the design decisions and find potential usability flaws via feedback from users. Then, implement changes and conduct summative usability study to find and fix usability issues in the final design.
