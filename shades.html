About the game that started it all

Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six: Vegas 2 is the seventh installment in the Rainbow Six series. It is a first-person shooter video game and the sequel to Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six: Vegas. I used to play this game with my friends a lot over our local network, and we wanted to have something portable that gives us the closest experience to this game, and we can utilize the portability of our phone and play anywhere.

Problem

There are thousands of shooting games on Google Play Store which can be played alone, with friends or online, but none of those games has as realistic AI system and mechanics as the high-end PC counterparts. Almost all such games are produced for the sole purpose of making money rather than giving joy to the player.

Solution

We created a top-down shooter for Android OS that has one of the smartest AI system that is aware of its surroundings, created an extensive list of achievements, unlockable items and ranks so there is always something to chase. Additionally, we made it playable as single-player, local network multiplayer and online multiplayer, providing complete flexibility to player which no other game provided at the time of its release. As a result this game had more than 1 Million downloads.

The Mechanics

List of Ranks and Unlocks

One of the first things was to figure out what selection of weapons will be default for players. So, I did some research to see what are some of the most popular weapons in some of the most popular games out there. Guns already available without any rank:

  1. Glock 17 (Handgun)

  2. Colt SCAMP (Handgun)

  3. FN P90 TR-10 (SMG)

  4. M61 Hand Grenade (Throwable)

Then the next thing was to figure out how many ranks will be there and what each rank will unlock. For this, I looked into the ranking and unlock system in some of the most popular shooting games out there. The most popular way to calculate rank was to use “XP” method, which is rewarded for every kill player, does in game or something special such as double kill. Ranks we decided upon:

  • Rank 1: 500XP
    Unlocks: None

  • Rank 2: 1250XP
    Unlocks: Taurus Raging Bull (Handgun)

  • Rank 3: 2500XP
    Unlocks: Taurus MT-40 (SMG) & Poison Grenade (Throwable)

  • Rank 4: 5000XP
    Unlocks: PKM (Assault Gun)

  • Rank 5: 10,000XP
    Unlocks: FAMAS F1 (Assault Gun)

  • Rank 6: 15,000XP
    Unlocks: M112 C4 Demolition Pack (Throwable) & Armsel Protecta (SMG)

  • Rank 7: 20,000XP
    Unlocks: Mossberg 590A1 Blackwater (Shotgun) and Heckler & Koch HK417 (Assault Gun)

  • Rank 8: 30,000XP
    Unlocks: Sawed-off Shotgun (Shotgun)

  • Rank 9: 40,000XP
    Unlocks: Milkor Mark 14 MGL (Heavy Gun)

  • Rank 10: 50,000XP
    Unlocks: Flamethrower (Heavy Gun) & Remington Model 870 (Shotgun)

  • Rank 11: 70,000XP
    Unlocks: RPG-7 (Heavy Gun)

  • Rank 12: 90,000XP
    Unlocks: 2000XP

  • Rank 13: 100,000XP
    Unlocks: 5000XP

List of Achievements

Keeping players engaged and motivated is a key part of having a good retention rate for a game or an app. One way of doing is to create Achievements that players can chase after. We also created an extensive list of 60 achievements, some of them are:

  1. Minute Man (Finish Carnage Level in Hard mode within a minute): 300XP (Single Player mode)

  2. Killer Beam (Kill 20 enemies in a row): 100XP (Single Player and Multiplayer mode)

  3. I was born with a gun (Complete a level without dying when game is played first time): 100XP (Single Player mode)

  4. Melee Miller (Kill 50 enemies with melee): 250XP (Single Player and Multiplayer mode)

  5. Resistance Revolution (Complete all levels in a row without taking a health pickup): 1250XP (Single Player mode)

  6. I’m an addict (Use 100 health pickups): 400XP (Single Player and Multiplayer mode)

  7. Secret Service (Kill all enemies in a level without alerting them): 2000XP (Single Player mode)

  8. The Uprising (Kill two players within 10 seconds of the start of the game): 3000XP (Multiplayer mode)

  9. Undead (Remain alive in a match) 2000XP (Multiplayer mode)

  10. Savior (Get killed with a buller fired on someone else): 500XP (Multiplayer mode)

The User Interface

A combination of dark and light scheme

We wanted all the CTAs to be very obvious and the entire interface to be clutter-free, so we kept the elements on screen to be minimum. We also added features like player customization, level previews, detailed scoreboards in multiplayer sessions and many more.

A game so fun to play, it spread like fire!

An updated UX to serve players even better.

After a lot of testing and playing in different environments, we found out that a dark UI is not very useful, and we also received the same feedback from hundreds of players from around the world. So, we decided to update the color scheme of the game towards blue, which is an ideal color for variable lighting environments.
