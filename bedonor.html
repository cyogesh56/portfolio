How Might We make the process of blood donation less intimidating and more rewarding so more people are inclined to donate?

Jan 2018 - Mar 2018

Range of work:

Research Review
Logo Design
UI Design

Challenge

The challenge for this project was to find out how some of the most successful non-profits are bringing donations through their websites and apps, and what design factors affect visitors’ decision on a non-profit website to engage or donate. Then use the research information to redesign an existing application.

Solution

I used one of my previous projects called “BeDonor” to redesign using research. For research review, I created a topic “Aesthetics and Social factors of Non-Profit Websites and Apps”, studied multiple peer-reviewed scholarly journals to find how non-profit websites and apps are engaging visitors and users to do certain tasks such as volunteering or donating. Then I used the information to redesign the UI of BeDonor.

About BeDonor

BeDonor is the name of a conceptual application I initially designed in 2014 for centralizing the blood donation process. The key idea was to democratize the process of not just donating blood, but also empowering both the receiver and blood banks to receive and manage blood and inventory. A year later, in 2015, I extended the design to further empower donors and receivers to go beyond simply just registering for blood donation and also find healthcare and emergency facilities, know the services they provide and also navigate to their location. Key features of BeDonor:

  • Ability to find, contact, connect and navigate to Blood Donors, Blood Banks and Healthcare facilities.

  • Ability to search by specific blood type on map and in a blood bank’s inventory.

  • Ability to arrange a pick-up or delivery of a specific amount of blood.

  • Detailed information about a healthcare facility’s amenities, services, ratings, reviews and contact information.

  • Stay engaged with Achievements, Volunteer opportunities and Physical Rewards after reaching a certain level.

Research Review

In 2018, I revisited the project as part of my academic goal to write a research review paper on “Aesthetics and Social Factors of Non-Profit Websites and Apps”. For this research paper, I read and reviewed peer-reviewed scholarly journals which tested various aesthetical factors that make non-profit websites more credible, trustworthy and compelling for visitors to engage and donate, and some social factors which generate satisfaction, motivation and trustworthiness.

Download Research Paper

Some of the highlights of research review:

  • Bright-warm colors and circular elements can create visual harmony and soothes users’ minds. If used properly, can increase engagement on the website.

  • Images of actual scenarios look attractive and influential. Humans get more engaged with real-life imagery than representative graphics.

  • Users need brief and most relevant information, in combination with visual cues such as icons and images.

  • Emojis can be used to provide feedback to users on their progress, and can even be used to gamify an experience.

  • By utilizing Gamification techniques, a brand can easily bring new users and increase engagements of users with their products.

  • Providing Rewards, be it digital or physical, to users for completing certain tasks can serve as a motivational factor for them to keep using the product and be engaged with it.

Creating an Evidence-based Design

Journey of Old to New

The old logo used heavy font and red color which could be intimidating and, the way it was designed, reduces its usage in places where a single-color logo can only be used. So, for the new logo, I used the same dot, but in a lighter color, and converted it to a very tender and calm looking logo.

A soothing logo animation

As seen in the design of the new logo, the construction took many shapes, from infinity to eight to a fan and a flower. To showcase multiple capabilities of this application, this transformative animation does appropriate justice.

A bright-warm color scheme

As found in research that bright-warm color schemes are more soothing to users’ eyes, I did some research to come up with a very less-intimidating version of red that is actually very appealing and tender. Then I also switched the font to Bahnschrift because it has more circular shape rather than rough and sharp edges.

New vs Old Comparison

The new version is significantly toned-down from being all red, dominating design to utilizing very calm-bright colors, rounded shapes and creating visual harmony on screen. For the new design, I followed iOS Design patterns, which are more flat and a little less confusing than material design.
