About Chicago Transit Authority

The Chicago Transit Authority (CTA) operates the nation's second largest public transportation system and covers the City of Chicago and 35 surrounding suburbs. On an average weekday, approximately 1.6 million rides are taken on the CTA.

Problem

Users are abandoning the Ventra app in favor of 3rd-party apps for transit information and navigation. They only use Ventra for managing their Ventra cards and passes. However, the intricate architecture and inefficient workflow of this app hinder users from efficiently doing so.

Solution

We researched the problems in the whole experience and created a mobile and a wearable application design that leverages the best heuristics of the best apps out there, and removed roadblocks to help users manage their Ventra Cards and Metra Passes conveniently.

Understanding what competition does best

“From the beginning we knew competition is far better, but competitive analysis helped us learn exactly what"

In our competitive review, we investigated several popular transit applications: Google Maps, Transit, Ventra, CityMapper, and Transit Stop. These applications had features that Ventra did not have, such as a map view, map and text view of routing, step by step commute information (e.g., est time to arrive, to walk, and disruptions). We noted down what each app does best, even the best features of Ventra itself.

Learning from users themselves

“We targeted students and working student professionals because those are the largest user base of Ventra app"

We wanted to know the experiences of everyday commuters with CTA trains and buses. Being commuters ourselves, we used our knowledge to create the questionnaire. The interviews helped us gather vital information about their commute problems and the reasons why people prefer 3rd-party apps over the Ventra app for transit information.

We also created a survey to get specific information from participants. Questions about their daily commute app, purpose of their commute, if they ever missed important announcements, etc. were asked in the survey. We gathered statistical evidence of the information we found through analysis, competitive review, and interview sessions.

Bringing together research to create Personas and Journey Maps

“The conversations we had with users of Ventra app gave us a very nutritious amount of information about what challenges are being faced by them"

One key finding in our research was that all the participants were using Ventra app, and this is because the ability to manage ventra passes is only available in ventra app. Simillarly, all of them used a different app to manage their daily commute. However, there was a difference in the way people commute and use these apps, so created two personas that informed us of the goals and frustrations of these two user types.

Designing and testing solutions

“We wanted Ventra app to be one-stop solutions for most of the user needs, which required it to be better at Usefulness and Findability of the features it provided"

We took the vanilla Ventra app which allowed users to manage their passes (found to be the most useful part in round 1 baseline usability test), enhanced it and extended the functionality of Ventra app to add the navigation capabilities. We tested each version starting from baseline vanilla Ventra app to sketches to mid-fi wireframes and hi-fi prototype. All along, constantly improving and keeping track of our most important KPIs:

  • Usefulness: The features the new app would provide should be something users need and provide the ability for them to complete their tasks.

  • Findability: The features the new app would provide should be easy to find; and not just the features, but the ability to change how the app works and behaves.

  • Ease of use: The new app should allow users to easily manage their commute and passes.

Improving Accessibility and Usability

“67 is a satisfactory score, but isn’t what should be of a real-world app that just got a redesign"

We learned about the issues with the hi-fi prototype during our usability test, which were with findability and usability of Ventra Pass management feature, which in the vanilla app was the most useful feature. And there were issues with color accessibility. So, to further enhance the new design, we worked on these and even further added new features such as a smartwatch app.
