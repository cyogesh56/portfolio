2009

What brought me here?

I always loved to design and since adolscence I’m filling up my notebooks and trimming-down pages to envision how the future of technology will look like. I'm always fascinated with technology and my entire life has revolved around them and I’ve spent 19 years without owning any of them. Owning my first feature phone (Nokia X2-01) had a huge impact on how I designed and what I designed.

2010

These were some of the things I skethced and filled my notebooks with. Smartphone technology was evolving and I was putting my thought into my sketches how multi-tasking would be done of future smartphone operating systems. At this point of time, I had no idea that this is called User Interface Design or a Wireframe but this pretty much laid the groundwork for me pursue the field of computing and design.

2012

When I got my first feature phone I saw the potential of creating content for those phones. So, I learned Photoshop on my own and started creating Themes for Nokia Store. This became the first source of income for me and I earned enough to buy myself a pair of Nike sneakers! Collaboration was always a motivator for me to create something and over the years I used it to create lots of amazing content.

Explore Past work on DeviantArt

2013

I got myself into learning 3D modeling and creating some next-level mobile phone concepts when Nokia closed it’s doors for individual creatives for it’s online App Store. I always believed Mobile is the Future, being an ultra-portable computing device.

2014

I’m always into creating something new, that has never been thought of. In that pursuit I learned so many things. Having done 3D modeling, I wanted to get into software apps and Photoshop was the first step for it. By 2014 I leanrned enough about it that I started designing lots of innovative app concepts and ResumeChef was one of them that I submitted as my Computer Science Capstone project. It was the most innovative and user-friendly interface among all submissions as said by the professor.

2015

Having learned Photoshop, I wanted to step-up my knowledge and learn a better design tool. This came with my first professional break! I was contacted by my friend who wanted to get a logo and branding design done for his textile company in Amsterdam. This project helped me learn Adobe Illustrator and create this one of my most beautiful piece of work to date.

“From heaven to deep sea, we have every fabric and color for you to see!"

Explore Project on Behance

2016

I always love to experiment and collaborate and this landed me on a super-hit project with my friend to create an Android game. We both were fans of games like Rainbow Six and Tekken, and wanted to have a game that we can play on the go, outside of our home. So, we co-created a top-down shooter called “Shades: Combat Militia” where I provided the UI design. By the end of 2019, it has 1,000,000+ downloads!

Very early-on I got annoyed with Instagram’s square cropping feature of every photo and that led me to join the profession photo sharing community EyeEm and since I joined it nearly 8 years ago, by the end of 2019, I had more than 1.1K followers on EyeEm.

2018

By this time I was in the mid of my Human Computer Interaction Master’s degree program and I wanted to explore design of physical experiences. So I collaborated with a couple of folks in my university to re-imagine how we create music, making it more collaborative, interactive and fun!

Explore Project on Behance

2019

Yet another collaboration to design physical experience for public. This year I collaborated with a couple of folks to create an Art Installation as part of my degree project. This was a machine that created artwork using your heart-beat on a piece of paper that you can take with you. This project got recognized as one of the most innovative projects in the University and got us featured on the University homepage!
