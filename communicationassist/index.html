Domain and Theme

The challenge was to create a communication interface or a device using radical technology. The first thing that came to my mind was Stephen Hawking. And, that’s when I decided that I would create something for people with ALS. As I started researching, I found that ALS is a part of a bigger hirearchy of diseases and comes under Motor Neuron Disease. All the diseases that come under MND lead to patients losing their ability to communicate.

Statistics of Motor Neuron Disease

  • Every 2 people per 100,000 are diagnosed with ALS.

  • Usually leads to death within 3-5 years.

  • Less than 5% survive more than 10 years.

  • Can only be diagnosed clinically.

Currently used MND communication methods

Currently, hospitals and caretakers are using hardware boards with important words written on them or computers with specialized hardware and software. A study from University of Los Angeles shows that these technologies have not so great satisfaction level. One reason is that it takes a lot of time to form sentences using these methods.

Competitive Review

There are many products in the market that can help people who lost their ability to speak, communicate with the world around them. Each one of them have some pros and cons which are listed in the picture below. The most commonly used item is a physical board called Vidatak EZ board which is always used as base for newer technologies. There are many other technologies such as Tobii Dynavox, Gaze Speak, Vidatalk, EyeSpeak, etc. Each produce provide some sort of unique functionality but all of them lack one most important feature: flexibility.

Problems with current assistive methods

  • Most of them suffer with inability to be used by the patient himself.

  • Figures not always represent the mental model.

  • Unprofessional look of interface elements.

  • Some of the methods are very Expensive.

  • Most of the experiences stand nowhere on usability standards.

  • Time consuming to form sentences.

Research Process

Pain Points of communication of people with ALS

  • Loss of motor functions such as ability to move body parts, speak, eat, sometimes over time and sometimes all at once, leading to inability to do everyday tasks.

  • Lots of communication issues due to loss of motor functions leading to intense level of frustration and sometimes depression at initial stages.

  • Medical equippments usually cost a fortune and can put even a well maintained family under debt.

  • Communication assistant devices are hard to learn since the patients cannot use their hands fully or partially.

Prototype Testing

After I completed the mid-fi prototype, I did another pilot test with a script to find out the best way of testing it with users since it’s extremely hard to find people with ALS given the timeframe I had to complete this project. Once I figured out all the logistics, I tested the prototype with 4 participants. That totals to 5 participants including a pilot test.

  • Speaking was not allowed.

  • Only one finger was allowed to use.

  • Gestures were not allowed.

  • Tried to mimic the actual scenario.

  • Follow-up questions.

Final Design

Final is just a word which is never achieved. There is always a room for improvement and nothing can be perfect. With that in mind, I have designed the Hi-Fi prototype and now I’m looking forward to test this with real patients with Motor Neurom Disease.

Identity Design

After completing the Mid-fi testing, I found confidence in the UX of this concept application. It was not time to improve the UI. So, I reimagined the interface and part of reimagination was to create a complete solution for a low-cost device. That’s why I decided to go a level up and create ScreenAssist.

ScreenAssist

ScreenAssist would be the overarching accessible operating system that can be easily used by not just ALS patients but also their caretakers and people with speech motor disabilities.

CommunicationAssist

CommunicationAssist would still be present in the whole ecosystem of accessible applications available to be installed in the ScreenAssist ecosystem. And, since the most important part of this project was communication, CommunicationAssist is the highlight, with a brand-new redesign.

Styleguide

Styleguide is a very important part of creating a consistent visual design and great user experience. I decided to make everything more prominent on screen by use of fonts, layout and color.

User Interface

The focus of designing this interface system was that it should be usable by not just the patients but also their caretakers and loved ones. That’s why all the interface elements are now relatively smaller but still big enough to be easily touched when using a touchscreen. Also big enough to be easily reachable by eye tracking and prominent enough to be used with a track-ball.

Loading Animation

A cleaner Interface

The focus of this iteration was to improve upton all of the previous and current technologies’ limitations in terms of Visual Design and User Experience. I focused to create simple and easy to identify icons and removed all the clutter from the home screen. Plus, the “Quick Select” is a customizable and persistent sidebar through which user can quickly communicate their favorite words no matter which app is open.

Taking on the feedback from usability study, the categories are now more prominent, a major improvement over any other technology. Using real-world imagery is much more easier to inderstand than using icons.
